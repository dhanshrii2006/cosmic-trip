Cosmic Knowledge

The universe is approximately 13.8 billion years old.
A day on Venus is longer than its year.
Neutron stars are so dense that a sugar-cube-sized amount would weigh billions of tons.
There’s a giant cloud of alcohol in space near the center of the Milky Way.
A spoonful of a neutron star weighs about a billion tons.
Saturn’s moon Titan has lakes of liquid methane and ethane.
There are more stars in the universe than grains of sand on all Earth’s beaches.
The coldest place in the universe is the Boomerang Nebula, at -457.7°F (-272°C).
Black holes can slow down time due to extreme gravitational pull.
Some exoplanets may have diamond rain due to extreme pressures.
